Sightseeing and culture

Old Town is full of important monuments, shops and restaurants and contains one of the most popular tourist routes. The centre of this part is called The Old Town Square and offers numerous prominent landmarks. The most popular is the unique Old Town Hall with the Astronomical Clock, St. Nicholas Church, the Church of Our Lady before Tyn, Kinsky Palace, House at the Stone Bell and the Jan Hus monument in the heart of the square. There are many significant buildings around the square including Klementinum, The Municipal Hall and The Powder Tower. The Old Town also takes in the Jewish Quarter, which currently manages five synagogues: the Maisel Synagogue, the Pinkas Synagogue, Spanish Synagogue, the Klausen Synagogue and the Ceremonial Hall of the Burial Society; and the Old Jewish Cemetery.

THEATRES AND MUSIC

There are three significant stages for ballet and opera lovers in Prague - The National Theatre, The Estate Theatre and The State Opera House. Another form of theatre with a long tradition is the Black Theatre, which uses striking combinations of lighting effects, music and actors' performances. Contemporary dance and movement performances can be seen in theatres: Ponec, La Fabrika or Archa. If you long for an extraordinary experience a visit to a puppet performance, which has a very long tradition in the Czech Republic, would not disappoint you. Many seasonal festivals are held in Prague throughout the year such as The Summer Shakespeare Festival. Prague classical concerts are staged in stunning concert halls, such Smetana's Hall or in Rudolfinum and churches located in the heart of the city and there are regular classic music festivals - The Prague Spring and Strings of Autumn. And you must certainly not forget O2 Arena (formerly known as the Sazka Arena) to watch a concert of one of your favourite contemporary bands.

MUSEUMS AND GALLERIES

The National Gallery has many branches in several places. Permanent exhibitions feature Medieval Art in Bohemia and Central Europe, 19th century art from classicism to romanticism, European Art from the Classical Era to the Baroque, Baroque in Bohemia, The Art of Asia and The Ancient World and Art of the 20th and 21st centuries with famous The Slavic Epic from Alfons Mucha. The privately owned Lobkowicz Palace is home of The Lobkowicz Collection with master works by Canaletto, Brueghel the Elder, Cranach and Velázquez, musical instruments and original scores and manuscripts by Beethoven and Mozart. There are many other galleries including Kampa Museum with its permanent exhibition of Modern Central European Art, Rudolfinum or DOX Gallery with modern art. More than a hundred of Museums can call Prague home. Anyone can choose for the vast array, for they are suitable for everyone from little children to older adults. Among the most popular are The Museum of Capital of Prague - which contains The Langweil model of the city, The National Museum, The Technical Museum, The Museum of Decorative Arts in Prague, Alfons Mucha Museum, Franz Kafka Museum, The Kingdom of Railways, Petřín Hill with mirror maze, The Museum of Public Transport's History, Planetarium, Czech Museum of Music, Dvorak's Museum, Smetana's Museum, Lapidary - the exhibition of Stone Monuments, Military History Institute and others.

Teambuildings and activities

SKODA/TRABANT RALLYE
This is a funny race in the Prague city street with Trabants - the old-fashion communists cars originally from East Germany. Clients are divided into smaller teams of three members. Each team gets map of Prague, road books, digital camera, trabant car history booklet and own Trabant car with driver. The aim of the rallye is to find three checkpoints and fulfill several tasks. On the last checkpoint there is time for driving lesson with trabants. You might try to drive car on your own or engage in other quests like loading the carriage, blind slalom or pushing the car without using the engine.

PRAGUE MOVIE MAKER
At the beginning of the program, there is short introduction of the activity and technical rules are explained. Group is then divided into several teams. Each movie crew contains director, screenwriter, actors and actresses, cameraman, lighting technical, clapboard girl, musician etc. The aim is to shoot at least 120 second commercial spot. Each team can use a wide range of costumes, wig collection and HD movie devices with iPads and MP3 music library. In the evening there is a screen projection of all movies and "Academy" gives out the prizes for The Best Male Actor, The Best Female Actor, The Best Movie, etc.

GO KARTS
Fun that is not suitable only for young people! We offer the real racing on the longest kart track in Europe, at 910 meters. There are more than 30 curves and average speed reaches as much as 40kmph. The track is situated in an old glasshouse which provides great light conditions and it is heated in winter and air-conditioned in summer.

BEER HUNT
This is an interactive tour for beer lovers including visits to famous pubs. Each team receives a game road book, map of Prague, envelope with money, digital camera and beer passport for collecting stamps from restaurants. The aim of the game is visit as much pubs marked on the map as possible and taste local beers. Funny photos from every pubs serve as evidence. There is also a quiz about beer and Prague included in the game.

BOAT CRUISES AND RIDES BY HISTORICAL TRAM
Most Prague tours are walking tour because Prague's urban plan consists of old narrow medieval streets where buses can hardly fit. Alternative means of transportation can be by historical tram or boat tours. The historical trams date back to 1908 - 1942 and have wooden interiors. The tram conductor wears a stylish historical uniform. A glass of sect can be arranged for guests. If you do not fancy tram ride, the boat cruise on Moldau River might be an option for you. From the river you can admire the most important monuments and have a buffet meal.

SHOPPING
During your visit you can purchase wonderful presents for yourself as well as for those close to you. Typical Czech products are traditional wooden toys for children, Jewellery from Bohemian garnets, natural cosmetics, Czech beer or wine, cut crystal, porcelain and Becherovka- the alcoholic herbal liqueur - and the highly alcoholic spirit called Absinth.

Dining

PUBS AND BREWERIES
Prague is popular for great beer. There are many typical pubs and small breweries here. The history of brewing in Prague dates back many centuries and small breweries with their own restaurants stil exist. Some produce different types of beer according to the season of the year. You can try many typical specialtie,s such as beer cheese, goulash or sausages made on beer but also Czech cuisine, which is based on sauces, meat and dumplings. So beer is necessary for digestion!

INTERNATIONAL RESTAURANTS
