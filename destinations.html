PRAGUE – THE PERFECT DESTINATION

Are you looking for a weekend break? Do you want to see breath-taking monuments, visit cultural events or experience adventurous trips? Are you looking for a perfect place for your conference or meeting? Prague is a perfect destination offering all of these. Prague offers magical atmosphere – you can stroll through history and at the same time enjoy modern shopping malls, galleries, museums or theatre performances or discover local cafes and restaurant and taste the world-famous beer. Prague is ideally located in the middle of Europe and can be reach easily from many points.

THE CZECH REPUBLIC

There are many interesting places outside of Prague. The Czech Republic is a small country but can be proud of 12 UNESCO monuments. West Bohemia is known for Spa cities with mineral healing springs offering Spa treatments. South Bohemia is renowned for its beautiful forrest landscape, abundance of ponds and fisheries and romantic towns, chateau and castles. South Moravia is region of notable wine production and warm climate. Close to Prague are numerous interesting places such as breweries (Pilsen), castles and chateaux (Karlstejn, Krivoklat, Konopiste), UNESCO monuments (Kutna Hora medieval town) and much more.Following excursions belong among the most popular ones.

WESTERN BOHEMIA WITH BEER SPA

Visit of West Bohemia with Chodova Plana's Spa - thanks to mineral springs and its own brewery you can enjoy an unusual bath mixture containing both of them. Afterwards you will visit Pilsner Urquell's brewery - the producer of worldwide famous beer.

VISIT OF SKODA CAR PRODUCTION

Only 4 car companies in the whole world have more then 100 years of production history without a break. One of them is Skoda car. You can visit and see present-day car production and also a museum where you can get to know history of Skoda cars from their beginnings until the present time and also see an exposition called “Sleeping beauty" dedicated to old-timer cars.

THE BIRTHPLACE OF THE REAL BEER - PILSNER URQUELL

Did you know that nine out of ten beers consumed in the World are derived from the original Pilsen Beer invented in the Czech Republic? A brewery tour lets you see beer production and taste this famous beer.

RÜCKL CRYSTAL GLASSWORKS

Producer of an extensive range of cut 24% leaded crystal in a wide variety of decorations. During the tour you will get to know the individual stages of producing glass crystal by hand, i.e. the furnaces, processing, designing and polishing, and you will have an opportunity to buy produced goods.

BOHEMIA SEKT FACTORY

You will learn how the bottle bearing The Bohemia Sekt logo comes to life or how the still wine or a fine sparkling wine is produced from renowned Moravian vineyards. You will be introduced to the alchemy of the production process and have explained a number of technical terms.

KUTNA HORA - UNESCO TOWN

Old mining town founded in the 13th century and designated by UNESCO as world heritage site. Visit gothic St. Barbara Cathedral and the former royal mint, which once coined Prague groschen. You can also visit a unique Ossuary made from human bones.

CASTLES AROUND PRAGUE

There are many castles around Prague worthy of your visit. You can choose from Konopiste, Krivoklat, Karlstejn Castles or Melnik Chateau where you can also visit wine cellars and have a wine tasting from local vineyard's.

SOUTH BOHEMIA EXPERIENCE

South Bohemia full day trip with visit to Jindrichuv Hradec, the castle founded in the 13th century which is the third largest castle complex in the Czech Republic. In the city museum visit Kryza's crib - the largest mechanical nativity scene in the world according to the Guinness Book- and on the way back visit of Tabor historical town.

WEST BOHEMIA WITH SPA CITIES

The westernmost region near the border with Germany is known for its Spas. The frequency of occurrence of healing mineral springs in the triangle between Karlovy Vary, Marianske Lazne and Frantiskovy Lazne is unparalleled in the world. Mineral springs are exceptional for their chemical composition and therefore for the range of physiological and therapeutic effects.

Bratislava and Slovakia

BRATISLAVA

The capital of Slovakia is nowadays the modern city and the seat of most important political, economic, social and scientific bodies and institutions of the Slovak Republic. The city is suitable for a long weekend, meeting or combination with visit of neighbouring countries. OUTSIDE OF BRATISLAVA Towns Modra and Pezinok stretch below the mountains of Male Karpaty in Western Slovakia about 45 km from Bratislava. They became known for their wine and vineyards and manufacturing of pottery called majolica. Devin Castle is located approximately 12 km from Bratislava. This impressive landmark, today used for exhibitions of the Bratislava City Museum, belongs to to oldest history of Slovakia.

Vienna and Austria

DAY TRIP TO VIENNA

Extraordinary experience can be a one- day trip to Vienna by boat. Hydrofoil express boat from Bratislava's downtown reaches Vienna in 100 minutes. A popular day trip offers an opportunity to discover natural beauties of the river Danube as well as the chance to spend a day in the centre of Austria's capital.

Krakow and Poland

KRAKOW

Krakow is the second largest city in Poland. It is an ancient, university city, cozy, full of bars, restaurants and historical monuments. The Old Town (Old Town) is listed on the UNESCO World Heritage List. OUTSIDE OF KRAKOW Another interesting places to visit are salt mines Wieliczka, also listed on UNESCO list and former concentration camp Oswiecim (Auschwitz).
